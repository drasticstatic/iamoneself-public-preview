A Sufi Message of Harmony, Love, and Beauty. Where differences come to an end.

About Chaiconi Bari

Chaiconi Bari is Shipibo-Conibo in origin. Chaiconi speaks to the wisdom Spirits invisible to human eyes but seen when the Spiritual Eye is activated — Spirits of the plants, ancestors, and shamans. Bari means a radiant circular energy field that nurtures and sustains life. Together, the term represents the Holy Spirit, the Mind of the Atonement where the Christ stands revealed in the Golden Halo.

The Eagle — Western Mind of Medicine

The Eagle represents the resurrected one who wrote A Course in Miracles — the Spirit of Yeshua. The Course is a self-study spiritual thought system that teaches that the way to universal love and peace is by undoing guilt through forgiving others. Its 76 Miracle Principles form the cognitive backbone of our teaching.

The Condor — Indigenous Mind of Plant Wisdom

The Condor is the Spirit of Ayahuasca, known as the Vine of the Soul and Cosmic Serpent. A great teacher of the Sacred Manuscript of Nature and the Jungle Book. For thousands of years, indigenous healers have worked with master plants as sacred allies in the healing of the mind and spirit.

The Barn Owl — The Two Become One

When the Eagle and the Condor fly together, they become the Barn Owl — symbolizing Single-Mindedness and seraphic teaching. The two teachings now live in harmony to heal mankind. Together they fly as one bird shining in the oneness of the Golden Halo.
